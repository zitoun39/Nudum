# Nudum Business Model

## Purpose

This document defines the commercial model, customer segments, pricing philosophy, revenue streams, deployment models, and long-term business strategy of the Nudum platform.

Its purpose is to ensure that product development aligns with sustainable business objectives while delivering measurable value to customers.

---

# Business Vision

Nudum is positioned as an **Enterprise Modular SaaS Platform** designed for organizations that require secure, scalable, and configurable software to manage operations, laboratories, quality assurance, document governance, and institutional workflows.

The platform is built around a modular subscription model that allows organizations to adopt only the capabilities they require while maintaining a unified digital ecosystem.

---

# Value Proposition

Nudum delivers value by replacing fragmented systems, spreadsheets, paper-based workflows, and isolated applications with one integrated platform.

Organizations benefit from:

* Centralized operations.
* Reduced manual work.
* Improved regulatory compliance.
* Faster access to information.
* Better collaboration.
* AI-assisted decision support.
* Lower software maintenance costs.
* Long-term scalability.

# Product Strategy

Nudum follows a **Modular Platform Strategy**.

Customers subscribe only to the modules they require.

Examples include:

* Mahattati
* Jawdati
* Archivi

Future modules may include:

* Maintenance
* Asset Management
* Procurement
* GIS
* HR
* Fleet Management
* Executive Dashboards
* AI Knowledge Assistant

The platform grows by activating modules—not by installing independent products.

---

# Revenue Model

Revenue is generated through multiple complementary streams.

## SaaS Subscription

Monthly or annual subscriptions.

Pricing depends on:

* Active modules
* Number of users
* Number of operational sites
* Storage
* AI consumption
* Support level

---

## On-Premise Licensing

Organizations may purchase annual licenses for local deployment.

Licensing includes:

* Updates
* Security patches
* Technical support

Infrastructure remains under customer control.

---

## Professional Services

Optional services include:

* Deployment
* Data migration
* Training
* Custom integrations
* Consulting
* Performance optimization

---

## Premium AI Services

Organizations may subscribe to advanced AI capabilities.

Examples:

* Intelligent document analysis
* Predictive analytics
* AI assistants
* Knowledge search
* Automated reporting

AI services may be billed separately based on consumption.

---

# Subscription Philosophy

Organizations should pay for value rather than complexity.

Pricing should remain:

* Transparent
* Predictable
* Scalable

Customers should never pay for unused modules.

---

# Deployment Models

Nudum supports multiple deployment strategies.

## Cloud SaaS

Default deployment model.

Ideal for most organizations.

---

## Private Cloud

Dedicated infrastructure for organizations with stricter governance requirements.

---

## On-Premise

Local deployment within customer infrastructure.

Suitable for organizations with regulatory or security constraints.

---

## Hybrid

Combination of local infrastructure and cloud services.

---

# Billing Strategy

Billing should support:

* Monthly subscriptions
* Annual subscriptions
* Multi-year contracts
* Enterprise agreements

Invoices should be generated automatically.

Organizations should be able to manage subscriptions directly through the platform.

---

# Payment Strategy

The platform should support regional and international payment methods.

## Regional Payments

Support should be added for payment providers available in target countries.

Examples may include:

* Local bank cards
* National payment gateways
* Bank transfers

---

## International Payments

Support should include international providers such as:

* Stripe
* Paddle
* Lemon Squeezy

The billing architecture must remain provider-independent.

---

# Licensing Philosophy

Customers own their operational data.

Nudum provides the software platform.

Organizations must always retain the ability to export their information using open formats.

Vendor lock-in is intentionally avoided.

---

# Customer Success

Long-term success depends on customer outcomes rather than software sales.

Success metrics include:

* Faster operations
* Reduced paperwork
* Improved compliance
* Higher laboratory efficiency
* Better document governance
* Increased user adoption
* Lower operational costs

Customer value drives business growth.

---

# Growth Strategy

Growth is expected through:

* New customers
* Additional modules
* AI services
* Strategic partnerships
* Regional expansion
* International SaaS deployment

The architecture should support growth without requiring product redesign.

---

# Competitive Position

Nudum differentiates itself through:

* Modular architecture
* AI-native capabilities
* Multilingual support
* Cloud and On-Premise deployment
* Enterprise security
* Open integrations
* Water-sector specialization
* Future extensibility beyond a single industry

The objective is not to compete by offering the largest number of features, but by delivering the most coherent and maintainable enterprise platform.

---

# Business Principles

Every commercial decision should reinforce the following principles:

* Deliver measurable customer value.
* Keep pricing transparent.
* Avoid unnecessary licensing complexity.
* Build long-term customer relationships.
* Protect customer data ownership.
* Support gradual digital transformation.
* Enable sustainable business growth.

---

# Business Model Statement

> **Nudum creates value by delivering a modular, intelligent, and secure enterprise platform that grows with its customers. Organizations subscribe only to the capabilities they need, retain full ownership of their data, and gain a long-term technology partner rather than a collection of disconnected software products.**
